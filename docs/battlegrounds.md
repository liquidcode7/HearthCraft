# HearthCraft — Battlegrounds (Lore Roster)

> Authoritative design document — June 2026.
> The fourteen named battlegrounds for the Campaign layer. Not built yet.
>
> Lore grounded against Tolkien canon (Appendices, Tale of Years, Tolkien
> Gateway). Where a battleground departs from book canon or leans on
> game-canon (LotRO etc.), it is flagged.

---

## The Concept

A "game mode" beside the everyday gather/cook/provision loop. A roster of
discrete, named battlegrounds drawn from the history of Middle-earth. Each is a
self-contained scenario you unlock and select into — not a campaign, not a
persistent place. A menu of escalating tests.

Your role never changes: you are the warlock-culinarian, the indispensable
support. In a battleground your provisioning is what lets a coalition force
attempt something at the edge of possibility. You are the Captain in the LotRO
sense — never the strongest in the room, but the reason the room holds together.

### Core structural ideas
- **Roster, not campaign.** A selectable list, not a linear march.
- **Selectable difficulty per battleground.** Each scenario scales from
  early-accessible to endgame. Higher difficulty = better rewards. One authored
  battleground serves the whole progression.
- **Gated by level + notoriety.** Higher battlegrounds unlock at thresholds of
  completion and reputation. The unlock is story-flavored and not loudly
  announced — discovery is part of the reward.
- **Seasons.** Rotating featured battlegrounds or seasonal modifiers keep it
  fresh without monetization. Offline, ad-free, never static.
- **Rare escalation event.** Top-tier battlegrounds carry a ~1-2% chance an
  ancient evil appears, turning a routine run into a real struggle. Implies
  resolution has built-in variance — the ancient evil is the tail of the
  distribution. Creates war stories.

### Two kinds of battleground (a useful distinction that emerged)
- **Hopeless defenses** — scenarios you are *meant to lose*, scored on how long
  and how well you hold. The Fall of Fornost, Helm's Deep before the dawn.
  Provisioning is what stretches an impossible stand.
- **Cleansings** — not pitched battles at all. Haunted or corrupted ground you
  restore (Barrow-downs, Dol Guldur). Light/druidic ritual flavor. The threat
  is the land itself, not an army.
- **Survive-and-repel** — an apex enemy you cannot kill, only outlast (the
  Witch-king at Fornost, by Glorfindel's prophecy).
- **Set-piece assaults / battles** — the classic large engagements (Pelennor,
  Carn Dûm, the Black Gate).

### Open mechanical question (unresolved)
When the ancient evil appears mid-battleground, can the player **respond** (cook
an emergency preparation, commit a reserve, pull a member back), or is the
outcome locked from pre-battle provisioning? This fork decides whether a
battleground is "prepare then resolve" or "actively played through." Decide
before building.

# THE ROSTER

Two broad eras. The **Angmar Wars** of the North (T.A. ~1409-1975) form an early
spine. The **War of the Ring** (T.A. 3018-3019) provides the famous high-tier
set-pieces. All threats keyed to the correct era — no anachronisms. Morgoth is
gone (cast into the Void, end of First Age) and appears nowhere. Avoid
Balrogs/First-Age horrors as "ancient evils"; use era-appropriate cold-drakes,
wight-lords, Nazgul, Mumakil.

## NORTHERN SPINE — The Angmar Wars

### 1. Cleansing of the Barrow-downs  *(Cleansing, early)*
**Tyrn Gorthad, Cardolan. Wights from ~T.A. 1636.**
- After the Dunedain of Cardolan were wiped out by the Great Plague, the
  Witch-king sent evil spirits from Angmar and Rhudaur to inhabit the barrows.
  They haunted the mounds for over a millennium.
- Threat is **undead** — barrow-wights (Norse-derived draugr). Haunted ground
  to cleanse, not an army. Perfect home for the cure-the-land / Light-ritual
  mechanic and the Druid Circle.
- **Rare escalation:** the wight in the barrow of the last prince of Cardolan.
- **Flavor hook:** Tom Bombadil's power here; the wights fear old songs.
- Canon: solid, book-canon (Fellowship; Appendix A).

### 2. The Fall of Fornost  *(Hopeless defense, mid)*
**Fornost Erain, North Downs, T.A. 1974.**
- NOTE: this is the *fall* (1974), not the reconquest (1975). The Witch-king's
  armies overran Arthedain, the last kingdom of Arnor; King Arvedui fled and
  later drowned in the Icebay of Forochel. A doomed last stand.
- You are provisioning a defense that cannot win — scored on how long the
  Dunedain hold, how many escape, what is saved. The opposite of a victory
  scenario.
- **Lore thread:** former soldiers of Arthedain were later cursed as shades for
  betraying Fornost to Angmar — a Dunharrow-style oathbreaker echo. Ties
  thematically to the Paths of the Dead (below).
- **Rare escalation:** the Witch-king himself on the walls.
- Canon: solid (Appendix A; Tale of Years).

### 3. Holding the Ettenmoors  *(Standing test, mid)*
**The troll-fells north of Rivendell, between Angmar and the Misty Mountains.**
- The contested borderland — the literal LotRO PvP region that inspired this
  whole mode. The "put yourself to the test" ground players grind at multiple
  difficulties.
- Wild, contested, full of trolls (it is the troll-fells) and Angmar raiding
  parties moving on the Dunedain marches.
- **Rare escalation:** an ancient stone-troll of the deep fells, or a captain
  of Carn Dum.
- Canon: place is book-canon; "holding the Ettenmoors" as a repeatable
  engagement is game-canon framing (LotRO). Flagged.

### 4. The Host of the West at Fornost  *(Set-piece battle, mid/high)*
**Plains between Lake Nenuial and the North Downs, T.A. 1975.**
- The reconquest — the victory that ended the Witch-realm. Gondor under Earnur,
  Elves of Lindon, Glorfindel from Rivendell, the last Dunedain under Aranarth.
- Won by a pincer: cavalry split into the Hills of Evendim and struck the
  Angmar host's rear. Model as a provisioning/timing challenge — the ambush
  wing must be sustained separately.
- **Survive-and-repel apex:** the Witch-king takes the field near the end,
  scattering horses with terror, and flees only at Glorfindel's arrival.
  Glorfindel's prophecy — "not by the hand of man will he fall" — means this is
  a boss you are NEVER meant to kill, only survive and drive off. Genuinely
  novel boss design.
- Canon: solid, book-canon.

### 5. Storming of Angmar / Assault on Carn Dum  *(Fortress assault, endgame-North)*
**Carn Dum, northernmost Misty Mountains, T.A. 1975 (just after Fornost).**
- The great northern fortress assault — the payoff of the northern spine. After
  Fornost the Host pressed on; Carn Dum was utterly destroyed and no Man or Orc
  of Angmar remained west of the Misty Mountains.
- The land: "not black and twisted like Mordor, but heartbreakingly cold and
  barren." A frozen citadel at the roof of the world. The **warmth** buff has
  an obvious mechanical home — surviving the cold is part of the assault. Home
  front for the Dwarven Company.
- **Rare escalation:** a great cold-drake of the northern wastes — era-correct
  "ancient evil," right scale, avoids First-Age anachronism.
- Canon: the fall of Angmar is book-canon; Carn Dum interior detail is largely
  RPG/game canon — flag if used.

## WAR OF THE RING — The Famous Set-pieces

### 6. The Paths of the Dead  *(Anti-battleground / summoning, high)*
**Dwimorberg to the Stone of Erech, White Mountains, T.A. 3019.**
- Not a fight. A passage through a haunted mountain road to summon an army
  through sheer dread. Aragorn, heir of Isildur, walks the Paths to call the
  Oathbreakers — Men of the Mountains cursed by Isildur for breaking their oath
  to fight Sauron, who can find no rest until an heir of Isildur summons them.
- A battleground about *nerve and endurance*, not combat. You provision a
  company to walk through terror itself and not break. Completely different
  texture from every other scenario.
- **Flavor:** the Grey Company; the Stone of Erech; Malbeth the Seer's
  prophecy. The land empties in fear before the King of the Dead.
- Canon: solid, book-canon (Return of the King; Appendix A).

### 7. Battle under the Trees / Mirkwood  *(Hopeless-leaning defense, high)*
**Thranduil's Woodland Realm, 15 March T.A. 3019.**
- On the same days as Pelennor, Dol Guldur's army assaulted Mirkwood. A long
  battle under the trees and "a great ruin of fire" among the woods — the
  forest literally burning. Thranduil was victorious in the end.
- A defense of living forest against fire and Orcs — high stakes, the home
  realm aflame. Strong elven/woodland flavor; resonates with the Druid Circle.
- Canon: solid, book-canon (Appendix B; Tale of Years).

### 8. The Cleansing of Dol Guldur  *(Cleansing, endgame)*
**Southern Mirkwood, 28 March - 6 April T.A. 3019.**
- The northern counterpart to the destruction of Barad-dur. Dol Guldur launched
  three assaults across the Anduin on Lorien, each thrown back by the power of
  Nenya. Then Celeborn led the Galadhrim across the river and took the fortress,
  and **Galadriel threw down its walls and laid bare its pits, cleansing the
  forest.** The wood was renamed Eryn Lasgalen, the Wood of Greenleaves.
- This is the Druid Circle's ultimate fantasy made canon: not just taking a
  fortress but *healing the land it poisoned*. The endgame cleansing.
- **Rare escalation:** Khamul, the Nazgul of Dol Guldur (the second of the Nine);
  or the lingering shadow of the Necromancer.
- Canon: solid, book-canon (Appendix B).

### 9. Helm's Deep / The Hornburg  *(Hopeless defense, high)*
**The Hornburg, Westfold of Rohan, night of 3-4 March T.A. 3019.**
- The archetypal hopeless defense. A vastly outnumbered garrison holds an
  ancient fortress through a desperate night against Saruman's Uruk-hai host,
  until dawn and Gandalf's arrival turn it. Scored on holding until dawn.
- Provisioning a stand that should not survive the night is the purest
  expression of the "hopeless defense" battleground type.
- **Rare escalation:** the Uruk-hai berserkers / the blasting-fire at the
  culvert (the breach of the Deeping Wall).
- Canon: solid, book-canon (The Two Towers).

### 10. The Battle of the Pelennor Fields  *(Grand set-piece, endgame)*
**Before the walls of Minas Tirith, 15 March T.A. 3019.**
- The largest battle of the War of the Ring. The siege of Minas Tirith; the
  charge of the Rohirrim; Theoden's fall; Eowyn and Merry against the Witch-king
  ("I am no man"); the arrival of Aragorn with the ships of Umbar. The hinge of
  the age.
- A sprawling, multi-phase battleground — strong candidate for the
  multi-stage / multi-member provisioning the endgame wants. Many fronts to
  keep supplied at once.
- **Survive-and-repel echo:** the Witch-king again — undefeatable by man, felled
  only by Eowyn and Merry together. The prophecy pays off here.
- **Rare escalation:** the Mumakil (living siege-towers); the Witch-king on his
  fell beast.
- Canon: solid, book-canon (Return of the King).

### 11. The Black Gate / Battle of the Morannon  *(Final hopeless gambit, apex endgame)*
**Before the Morannon of Mordor, 25 March T.A. 3019.**
- The last battle — the Host of the West marches on the Black Gate as a
  deliberate, hopeless diversion to draw Sauron's Eye from Frodo. Outnumbered,
  expecting to die, buying time with their lives.
- The ultimate "hopeless defense" inverted into a hopeless *offense*. The whole
  point is that victory is not the objective — endurance and distraction are.
  Thematically the perfect capstone for a support-class hero: you keep a doomed
  army standing for someone else's sake.
- **Flavor:** the Mouth of Sauron; Aragorn's host; the eagles; the Ring's
  destruction ending it from afar.
- **Rare escalation:** a Nazgul descends; trolls of the Black Gate.
- Canon: solid, book-canon (Return of the King).

## Band -> Battleground Affinities (flavor, not lock-in)

- **Druid Circle** — Barrow-downs, Mirkwood, Dol Guldur (cleansing, living land)
- **Greycloaks** — Ettenmoors, both Fornosts, Paths of the Dead (wardens,
  borderlands, the haunted North; they mirror the Rangers/Dunedain)
- **Dwarven Company** — Carn Dum, Helm's Deep (mountain fortress, siege, stone)
- **Corsair Fleet** — the Harad/Umbar strand; thematically also Pelennor (the
  ships of Umbar that turn the battle) and the coastal approach

Any band can attempt any battleground; each has a "home front" where its
identity resonates.

## DWARVEN & NORTHERN STRAND (different ages, Dwarven Company home front)

### 12. Battle of Dale / Siege of Erebor  *(Hopeless-leaning defense, high)*
**Dale and the Gate of Erebor, 14-27 March T.A. 3019.**
- The decisive battle of the War of the Ring's *northern* theatre, fought on
  the same days as Pelennor and Mirkwood. Sauron sent a host of Easterlings
  from Rhun to keep the North from aiding Gondor. King Brand of Dale and King
  Dain Ironfoot of Erebor marched out, were driven back to the Gate, and both
  fell — Dain died defending Brand's body. The Gate of Erebor was never taken.
  The defenders held under siege until news of Sauron's fall broke the
  Easterlings, and Thorin III Stonehelm and Bard II drove them out.
- THE HOOK: Gandalf said that even victory at Pelennor would mean nothing if
  Dale fell — the enemy would then have ravaged Eriador and Rivendell. This is
  the battle nobody sings about that quietly saved everything. The player
  fights the unseen battle the famous one depended on.
- Strong Dwarven Company home front. A siege you hold, scored on survival until
  the larger war turns.
- **Rare escalation:** the Easterling war-machines / a captain of Rhun.
- Canon: solid, book-canon (Return of the King, Appendix B).

### 13. Battle of Azanulbizar  *(Pyrrhic assault, high — Misty Mountains)*
**Dimrill Dale (Nanduhirion), below the East-gate of Moria, T.A. 2799.**
- The bloodiest battle in Dwarven history and the climax of the War of the
  Dwarves and Orcs. After Azog murdered and defiled King Thror, the Dwarves
  spent six years sacking every Orc-hold from Gundabad to the Gladden, until
  only Moria was left. The final battle was fought on a sunless winter day in
  the valley below the East-gate — the same gate the Fellowship would later
  exit. A decisive but pyrrhic victory: half the Dwarven host dead or dying.
  Young Dain Ironfoot slew Azog; Thorin earned the name Oakenshield.
- THE DREAD: after the victory, Dain looked through the East-gate and saw
  Durin's Bane — the Balrog — and the Dwarves, even triumphant, dared not enter
  Moria. That unseen horror behind the gate is a perfect ambient threat: a
  presence you must never provoke, sitting at the edge of the battleground.
- The ultimate Dwarven Company battleground — vengeance, cost, and the line of
  Durin. Scored on victory *and* on how many you bring home alive (the cost is
  the point).
- **Rare escalation:** Durin's Bane stirs. You cannot fight it. You can only
  get your people out before it wakes fully — a survive-and-flee apex.
- Canon: solid, book-canon (Appendix A; Appendix B).

### 14. The Fall of Gondolin  *(Mythic hopeless defense / evacuation, apex)*
**Gondolin, the Encircling Mountains of Beleriand, First Age 510-511.**
- NOTE: FIRST AGE. ~6,000 years before everything else, and a deliberately
  different power-register. This is the age of MORGOTH himself (Sauron's
  master) and his full might. Here Balrogs (plural) and dragons led by Glaurung
  are not "ancient evil" rare events — they are the baseline assaulting army.
  Gondolin should be presented as a mythic, almost legendary battleground,
  separate in tone and scale from the rest. The deep end of the deep end.
- The hidden Elven city, betrayed from within by Maeglin (captured by Morgoth,
  he revealed the city's location and defences), attacked during the festival
  of the Gates of Summer with its guard lowered. King Turgon fell with his
  tower. It was unwinnable.
- YOUR ROLE: this is the purest hopeless defense — you do not save the city,
  you provision the EVACUATION. Tuor and Idril led the survivors out through a
  secret passage Idril had built. Among the refugees: the child Earendil, who
  will one day carry the light that brings Morgoth down. Success is measured
  purely in survivors. You keep a doomed people alive long enough for the
  future of the world to walk out of a burning city.
- THE THREAD: Glorfindel died here, fighting a Balrog alone in the mountain
  pass to protect the refugees, falling with it into the abyss. This is the
  SAME Glorfindel (returned from the dead) who drives off the Witch-king at
  Fornost thousands of years later. The hero of your middle battleground died
  saving the refugees of your mythic one. A connection lore fans will treasure.
- **Apex threat:** Gothmog, Lord of Balrogs; Glaurung, Father of Dragons.
- Canon: solid, book-canon (The Silmarillion; The Fall of Gondolin).

## Scope Reality Check
V3/V4 territory. Exists only if V1 ships and the core loop is fun, then alchemy
+ multi-member provisioning land in V2. Captured here so the destination is
known and can quietly inform architecture: keep mission resolution extensible
toward multi-member, multi-stage, variance-based outcomes, and toward
scoring partial/hopeless outcomes (hold-time, survivors) not just pass/fail.
Do not let it pull focus from V1.
