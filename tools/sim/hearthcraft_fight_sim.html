Encounter & Enemy

Encounter type ⓘ

Attrition = a normal boss you must kill before time runs out. Survival timer = an un-killable enemy (the Nine): you can't damage them, only endure until the clock ends.
L1–9: 20–30 min recommended  ·  L10–20: 30–45 min recommended

The enemy ⓘ

Boss resolve = damage to grind through to win. Steady drain = constant pressure your food offsets. Spikes = bursts that drive members to zero (when the Keeper must rescue).
interval is randomised ±50% each spike, damage ±30% — same mean, real variance. Fragile members (Fighter/Keeper) can catch a bad run of spikes with no mid-fight reaction possible.

Physical mitigation (armor) ⓘ

Armor eats martial damage. Penetration comes from the party’s draught potency (set in Provisioning below) — not from character stats. Magic damage (Keeper, Captain’s Will half) bypasses armor entirely.

Enemy statuses ⓘ

Toggle the debuffs/hazards this enemy inflicts. Dread suppresses damage (countered by Captain's Will + Hope). Shadow drains Will+Fate over time (countered by Radiance) — only certain foes carry it (great spiders, Ringwraiths, Balrog). Cold/Heat/Disease/Wakefulness press Morale (countered by Warmth/Heat-ease/Hale/Alert).

Provisioning — draught ⓘ

One draught for the whole party. Potency strips armor from physical damage — the only way to crack armored encounters. Leave at 0 if no potency draught equipped. Entry-tier potency draught: ~45. Mid-tier: ~65.

Provisioning — antidotes ⓘ

Check the draught antidotes you provisioned. Each answers its matching hazard status. Set strength to represent the draught’s tier.
Band & Provisioning

Provisioning — food level ⓘ

Cooking level sets food quality (HP/s). Each member also eats a recipe that boosts their primary stat — pick the recipe that suits their role.
→ 10.0 HP/s per member

The band ⓘ

Band level scales every member's stats up from their 1–20 starting spread. Each role is a fixed template: Warden tanky, Fighter fragile striker, Keeper fragile linchpin, Captain durable empowerer. Live stats shown below.

Wounds & the Keeper ⓘ

Zero Morale = one wound (keep fighting). 5 wounds = grievous = out, needs the Houses of Healing. The Keeper burst-rescues whoever hits zero (one at a time) and deals magic damage otherwise.
base burst scales with Keeper's Will

Sim variance ⓘ

DPS jitter adds a random ± wobble to each tick’s damage output. 0 = fully deterministic (every fight identical). 10% is a light wobble. 20%+ creates wide outcome variance. Affects win-rate curves — retune encounters when changing this.

Borin Ironmantle Warden
Never complained once in forty years. Reliable as bedrock. Ask how he is and he says "fine," means it, and is back at work before you finish asking. The band does not talk about what they owe him. They know.
The wall at the front. High Might and Vitality mean he soaks the most punishment and lasts the longest. His contribution to bringing an enemy down is modest — that is not his purpose. His purpose is to still be standing when others would not be.

He is the only member who can step between a killing blow and the Keeper. He will do this without being asked, and he will do it as many times as he is able.
✦ The Horn of Gondor
When the line is breaking and two or more are in peril, Borin has been known to blow a great horn — and for a time, every blow meant for another lands on him instead. He does not explain why he has such a horn, or where it came from. He does not have to.
Mira Fighter
Grew up among the Greycloaks. Has known Aldric since she was small. There is nothing uncertain about who she is or what she is for — she has always known, and that quiet certainty is not arrogance, it is inheritance. Notices things she does not mention until exactly the right moment.
The band's primary blade. Her Agility drives most of the damage that kills the enemy, and her penetration is what strips armor from anything wearing it — no other member comes close. She carries none of Borin's durability, and a spike that finds her is genuinely dangerous.

Feed her well. She cannot do her work from the floor.
✦ Black Arrow
When the fight is turning against the band and the kill seems out of reach, Mira has once or twice drawn something from her quiver that is not an ordinary arrow. What follows is not ordinary either. Against something killable, a significant piece of it simply stops fighting. Against something that cannot be killed, the shot buys time — the terror flinches, and the clock runs shorter.
Cael Keeper
Has kept a field journal since boyhood — threats, remedies, routes, lore reaching back to the Elder Days. Every member of the company has borrowed it at some point, usually without asking. They tease him about it the way you tease family. He is the memory of this company and he carries that with quiet pride.
The reason the others are still standing. His Will determines both how hard he hits and — far more importantly — how much he restores when a comrade hits zero. He has five of these interventions in him per fight. He uses each one carefully. Losing Cael is the beginning of the spiral; there is no one else who can pull a fallen member back.

Borin guards him from killing blows. That arrangement is not accidental.
✦ Laurelin's Grace
Occasionally — rarely — when a member should be done, grievously wounded and out of the fight, Cael calls on something beyond ordinary skill. The wounds reset. The member stands again. It has happened only a handful of times. Cael does not take credit for it, and the others have learned not to ask.
Aelindra Captain
Ancient and precise. Speaks as though each word costs something. Has outlived three forests and is not sentimental about it. Her stillness in a bad moment steadies everyone around her — not because she says anything, but because she does not move.
Durable where the Fighter is fragile, steadying where the Keeper is singular. Her Will and Fate feed into the band's resistance to Dread — the cowering of courage before evil. Against enemies that carry fear as a weapon, she is the difference between a band that fights and one that breaks.

Her damage is secondary. Her presence is not.
✦ Wrath, Ruin, and the Red Dawn
In the worst moments — when the line is cracking and two or more are in peril — Aelindra has been known to say something. Nobody remembers the words exactly. What they remember is the feeling: that the world had not ended yet, that there was still something worth fighting for, and that they were on their feet again. All of them. Every Inspiration that follows in the next half-minute seems to come easier.
