# HearthCraft — Bestiary & Enemy Tiers (Design Scratchpad)

> SCRATCHPAD. NOT V1. Far-future (battleground raid RPG, V5+). Captured so the
> design isn't lost. Do not build from this without promotion to docs/.
> Companion files: battlegrounds.md (the roster), battlegrounds-rpg.md (systems).
> Lore grounded against Tolkien canon. Tier names use canon terms where possible.

---

## The Core Idea

Enemies are ranked on a single power scale, and **inspiration's effect is a
function of the power gap** between the inspiring captain and the enemy. A
captain far above an enemy routs them; near their level, defeats them at cost;
below their level, can only survive and repel. Two properties are tracked
SEPARATELY:

- **Power** — how mighty the enemy is (how hard to overcome at all).
- **Untouchability** — whether it can be *killed* or only *repelled*, regardless
  of power. (A Balrog is mightier than a single Ringwraith but CAN be killed;
  the Nine cannot be killed by anyone, ever, no matter how mighty the captain —
  canon protects them until the Ring is unmade.)

This separation is the heart of the system and must be preserved.

## The Five Tiers

A litany of rising dread: **Rabble — the Fell — Banes — the Nine — Nameless Things.**
Three names are pulled directly from Tolkien's own vocabulary.

### 1. Rabble  *(inspiration DISMAYS them — they do NOT flee)*
Numerous, low. Orcs are driven, whipped forward, more afraid of their masters
than of any hero — they do NOT rout at the sight of a captain. Inspiration
DISMAYS them: they falter, quail, fall into confusion, fight at a fraction of
strength — but STILL FIGHT, and are easily cut down. (Théoden at Pelennor: the
host was thrown into dismay but kept fighting; the true mindless rout came only
later, when Sauron's will was removed.) Win condition: kill.
- Orcs, snaga, goblins
- Wargs, wild wolves
- Brigands, corrupt men of low rank
- Crebain (spy-birds)
- Lesser Mirkwood spiders (in small numbers)
- Common barrow-wights
- Inspiration effect: FULL dismay — they fight at a fraction of strength and
  are easily cut down, but do NOT flee. Any captain.

### 2. the Fell  *(inspiration DEFEATS them reliably)*
Stronger; they don't flee in terror, but an inspired hero overwhelms them.
"A troll vs. an inspired hero is a no-brainer." Win condition: kill.
- Cave trolls, hill trolls, stone-trolls
- Olog-hai (Sauron's greater trolls)
- Uruk-hai and Uruk captains
- Greater Mirkwood spiders
- **Cargûl** (the lesser red wraiths — LotRO-derived; knockoff wraiths,
  defeatable, nowhere near the true Nine in dread; they correctly live HERE)
- Wight-lords
- Mûmakil / Oliphaunts (war-beasts — huge, terrifying, but ultimately beasts)
- Half-trolls of Far Harad, corrupt men of rank
- Inspiration effect: STRONG. They fall. Any reasonable captain.

### 3. Banes  *(killable ONLY by an insanely powerful captain, AT COST)*
The mightiest things you can actually defeat. "Bane" is canon — Durin's Bane is
the Balrog; a Bane is mighty enough to end a people. Win condition: kill, but
only via inspiration from a peak-tier captain, and the deed MAY take the captain
with it (the Gandalf clause — he "died" defeating the Balrog). Rarest, richest
victory in the game.
- Balrogs (Maiar of fire and shadow)
- Shelob and creatures of her order (ancient, pre-Sauron, near-Maia in dread —
  "there before Sauron, and before the first stone of Barad-dûr")
- The great wolves (Carcharoth-types; werewolves of Sauron's breeding,
  Draugluin-types)
- Cold-drakes and lesser dragons (Scatha-types, drakes of the Grey Mountains)
- Inspiration effect: ONLY a peak-tier captain's inspiration reaches them, and
  at the risk of the captain's life. Ordinary inspiration does nothing.

### 4. the Nine  *(NEVER killed — only REPELLED)*
The Ringwraiths. Canon-protected: they cannot be destroyed by anyone until the
Ring is unmade. Win condition: SURVIVE the duration and DRIVE THEM OFF. They
withdraw and can return on future runs. This is a different win condition, not
an unwinnable wall — fully clearable, fully rewarding (among the richest clears
in the game, because surviving the Nine is among the hardest things possible).
- Exactly the Nine Ringwraiths — a specific, finite, named set. This tier holds
  ONLY them. (Generic lesser wraiths are Cargûl, down in the Fell.)
- Inspiration effect: cannot kill them regardless of captain power. A truly
  insane captain's inspiration can help drive one off faster / more surely, but
  never destroys. Drama is endurance, not a kill.
- Their dread is precisely that your power never *solves* them. Gandalf feared
  facing the Witch-king not because the Witch-king out-powered the Balrog that
  nearly killed him, but because the encounter had no clean victory available.

### 5. Nameless Things  *(NOT a combat tier — a very rare HAZARD)*
Gandalf's own phrase: the things that gnaw the deep, "older than Sauron," that
"even Sauron knows not." NOT enemies, NOT a combat tier, NOT a standard
escalation. A hazard mechanic only, governed by strict rules (below).
- Ungoliant (primeval light-devourer; Morgoth's own Balrogs drove her off)
- The Nameless Things beneath the deep places
- (First-Age world-enders like Glaurung/Ancalagon belong here too as pure dread,
  not as fightable content)

**RULES that keep the Nameless from breaking the game (all mandatory):**
- VERY, VERY RARE. Most players meet one a handful of times in the life of their
  game, if ever. A thing whispered about more than seen.
- A KNOWN PROPERTY of specific deepest-endgame battlegrounds — never a random
  surprise that voids a run.
- SIGNPOSTED in advance (e.g. "linger too long in the deep and you wake
  something older than the Enemy").
- AVOIDABLE BY GOOD PLAY — beaten by speed and not provoking it, never by
  fighting. The Moria feeling: flee, don't fight.
- NEVER the reward gate. Clearing is always done against beatable enemies; the
  Nameless is the doom you race, not the boss you must beat.
- Functions as a "timer with teeth" / doom-clock, not a combatant.

## How Win Conditions Vary by Tier (design payoff)

The bestiary gives battlegrounds OBJECTIVE VARIETY, not just difficulty:
- Rabble/Fell/Banes battlegrounds → KILL objectives (clear the field).
- the Nine battlegrounds → REPEL/SURVIVE objectives (endure, drive off).
- Nameless hazard battlegrounds → RACE objectives (clear fast, don't provoke).

This means not every raid is "grind enemy pool to zero," which keeps the roster
fresh and demands different provisioning/composition per objective type.

---

## Mixed Fields

The common case is a mixed field (e.g. an orc host LED by a Ringwraith). When
inspiration fires:
- It routs the Rabble and defeats the Fell on the field...
- ...but does NOTHING to the Nine wraith leading them.
- Result: your rally clears the rabble, and now you face the wraith with the
  field cleared but the real problem still standing. Inspiration changes the
  SHAPE of the fight without solving it. This is the intended, interesting case.

---

## Rewards by Apex Tier

- **the Nine (repel):** among the richest clears — rare ingredients, gear,
  legendaries, big notoriety + influence-map swing. You're rewarded for the
  CLEAR, and a repel IS a clear.
- **Banes (kill):** one of the rarest, richest payouts in the game — mythic-tier
  legendary, huge notoriety/influence swing — because the kill is a peak
  achievement that may cost the captain who made it.
- **Nameless (survive/escape):** the reward is survival and whatever the deep
  battleground itself yields; the Nameless is never the gate, so its "reward" is
  simply that you got out with the loot.

---

## Invocation Frequency (so apex threats stay meaningful)

- Apex enemies (Banes, the Nine) are FEATURED enemies of specific high-tier
  battlegrounds — you know where they are and CHOOSE to face them.
- PLUS the ~1–2% rare escalation chance on lower battlegrounds (an apex threat
  unexpectedly appears, turning a routine run dire).
- Nameless Things: rarest of all — a property of maybe one or two endgame
  battlegrounds, and even there only sometimes invoked.
- If apex threats appeared often they'd be trivialized or rage-inducing. Rare,
  chosen, marquee encounters = aspirational content.
